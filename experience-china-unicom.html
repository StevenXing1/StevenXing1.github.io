Project Overview

During my internship at China Unicom's Yuanjing AI Lab, I contributed to the development and optimization of large language models specifically designed for telecommunications domain applications. My work spanned across three critical areas: instructional prompt engineering, large-scale data curation and labeling, and comprehensive model evaluation research.

The primary goal was to enhance the model's ability to accurately respond to telecom-specific queries while minimizing hallucinations and improving overall answer relevance. This involved systematic experimentation with prompt engineering techniques, rigorous data quality control, and extensive benchmark testing across multiple model architectures.

Research Areas

Large-Scale Data Curation

Detailed Contributions

LLM Pre-training Setup & Data Pipeline

Participated in the pre-training setup and data pipeline development for domain-specific language models, with a focus on the telecom industry. Key responsibilities included:

  • Pre-training Data Preparation: Processed and cleaned large-scale telecommunications corpora, ensuring data quality and removing potential contamination sources that could affect benchmark validity.
  • Instruction-Following Dataset Design: Designed and implemented instruction-following datasets specifically tailored for telecom customer service scenarios, technical support queries, and network troubleshooting.
  • Training Data Quality Control: Established rigorous data validation pipelines to ensure consistency and accuracy across training datasets, directly contributing to improved model performance after retraining with curated data (15% perplexity reduction from 4.8 to 4.1).
  • Evaluation Protocol Development: Developed evaluation protocols to assess model performance across various dimensions including factual accuracy, response coherence, and domain expertise.

Data Labeling & Quality Control

Managed large-scale data labeling operations for training and evaluation datasets:

  • Annotation Framework Design: Developed comprehensive labeling guidelines for telecom-specific QA pairs, ensuring consistency across multiple annotators and data sources.
  • Quality Metrics: Established inter-annotator agreement protocols and quality benchmarks, maintaining high standards across 2.3 million labeled examples.
  • Domain Expertise Integration: Collaborated with telecommunications subject matter experts to validate technical accuracy and ensure proper coverage of industry-specific terminology and concepts.
  • Continuous Improvement: Implemented feedback loops to iteratively refine labeling criteria based on model performance and real-world usage patterns.

Instructional Prompting Research

Conducted extensive research on prompt engineering techniques to maximize model effectiveness:

  • Systematic Experimentation: Designed and executed A/B tests across 150+ prompt variations, methodically analyzing the impact of different instruction formats, context lengths, and framing strategies.
  • Prompt Templates: Developed reusable prompt templates optimized for various task categories including factual retrieval, reasoning-intensive queries, and multi-step problem solving.
  • Hallucination Mitigation: Identified and implemented prompting strategies that reduced model hallucinations by 12%, including explicit constraints, verification prompts, and uncertainty expression.
  • Performance Analysis: Established metrics and evaluation frameworks to quantitatively assess prompt effectiveness, leading to measurable improvements in answer relevance (+17%).

Key Insight

Through systematic prompt engineering, I discovered that carefully structured instructions with explicit task decomposition and constraint specification significantly improved model reliability in telecom-specific scenarios, reducing off-topic responses and improving technical accuracy.

Model Benchmarking & Analysis

Conducted comprehensive evaluation research comparing model performance across industry-standard benchmarks:

  • Multi-Model Comparison: Evaluated performance of Qwen2-Math (1.5B, 7B, 72B variants), Meta-Llama models, DeepSeek-Math, and other instruction-tuned architectures across diverse benchmarks.
  • Task Diversity: Assessed model capabilities across mathematical reasoning (GSM8K, MATH, Minerva Math), competition-level problems (AIME24, AMC23), multilingual tasks (GaoKao, CMATH), and domain-specific challenges.
  • Data Contamination Analysis: Implemented rigorous testing protocols to ensure benchmark validity, including 13-gram duplicate detection and training data decontamination procedures to maintain evaluation integrity.
  • Scaling Insights: Analyzed how model size affects performance across different task categories, providing actionable insights for model selection and deployment strategies in production environments.

Research Contribution

My benchmark analysis revealed critical insights about model performance trade-offs, particularly highlighting the superior performance of Qwen2-Math-72B-Instruct on complex reasoning tasks while identifying optimal model sizes for different deployment scenarios considering computational constraints and accuracy requirements.

  • Integration & Adoption: Successfully integrated the harness into production workflows across multiple teams, standardizing evaluation protocols and enabling consistent cross-model comparisons.
